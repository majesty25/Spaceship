Supporting Private Schools in Ghana: A Collaborative Approach for Educational Excellence

The Molex Foundation Africa received a gracious invitation to participate in the Annual Conference of the Ghana National Association of Private Schools, held in Abura Asebu Kwamankese at Moree. The distinguished gathering was chaired by Dr. Patrick Essien, the CEO of Molex Foundation Africa. In his keynote address, Dr. Essien emphasized the critical need for government support for economically accessible private schools, recognizing their significant role in advancing education in Ghana. He stressed that the absence of these private educational institutions would worsen overcrowding issues in public schools, highlighting the imperative of fostering collaboration among a diverse array of stakeholders, including traditional leaders, religious bodies, NGOs, and philanthropic organizations.

Furthermore, Dr. Essien issued a passionate call to educators in both private and public schools, urging them to actively promote the inclusion of girls in subjects traditionally considered male-dominated. He emphasized the importance of the government and the Ghana Education Service (GES) considering the intensity and duration of the Basic Education Certificate Examination (BECE), particularly concerning adolescent girls who may experience menstruation during the examination period.

Dr. Essien also offered valuable guidance, encouraging school proprietors to expand their focus beyond profit-making and to embrace a more profound societal and national impact. He recommended the implementation of regular career-oriented programs in basic schools, highlighting their potential to help students understand the real-world applications of their classroom learning and envision their future paths. Dr. Essien, a Career Coach and CEO of Molex Foundation, pledged that the foundation would provide its expertise at no cost to assist schools in developing career programs that would shape the future of their students, aligning with the foundation's motto: "Giving Back to Society."

In conclusion, Dr. Essien seized the opportunity to unveil an exciting initiative: the upcoming SHEROES STEM Camp, scheduled for 2024. This initiative exemplifies the foundation's steadfast commitment to giving back to society and promoting educationalÂ advancement.
